3. Real-Time Traffic Signal Timing Optimization

  • SDG 13 (Climate Action): Lowers emissions from idling industrial trucks.
  • Employs Max-Flow Algorithms to balance vehicle density across city grids.
  • Adjusts green-light intervals in real-time based on live traffic camera feeds.

    DAA Project Reflection

    Motivation & Problem Solving

    These industrial ideas were motivated by the "Static Inefficiency" of modern cities. Traditional waste routes and traffic signals follow fixed schedules, which is inefficient. By applying Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA), we can move from static systems to dynamic, self-optimizing ecosystems that mirror the efficiency of natural patterns.

    System Efficiency (Time & Space)

    Time Complexity: Crucial for Traffic and Safety. A chemical leak alert must be processed in $O(1)$ or $O(\log n)$ time using a Priority Queue; an $O(n^2)$ search could lead to disaster.

    Space Complexity: Important for Land Allocation. Storing massive spatial maps requires optimized data structures like Quad-trees or Segment Trees to save memory.

    Data Organization Strategy

    For Waste Management, we utilize Adjacency Lists for graphs because city maps are typically sparse. For Land Allocation, we use 2D Arrays to represent coordinates. For Chemical Safety, a Min-Heap ensures the highest priority emergency is always handled first.

    Algorithmic Techniques Used

    • Greedy: Finding the next nearest pickup point for waste trucks.
    • Backtracking: Fitting different sized industrial plots into a fixed land grid.
    • Divide & Conquer: Segmenting large traffic networks into smaller, manageable zones.
    • Shortest Path (Dijkstra): Emergency vehicle routing through smart traffic grids.

    Algorithmic Implementation

    Industrial Logic & DAA Implementation

    1. Pathfinding (Waste & Traffic)

    Dijkstra's Algorithm: Used to calculate the least-cost path for waste collection vehicles, considering distance and traffic load as edge weights.

    2. Spatial Optimization (Land Allocation)

    Backtracking / Bin Packing: A recursive approach that tries to place industrial plots of size $X \times Y$ into a larger grid, backtracking when a collision occurs.

    3. Emergency Processing (Chemical Safety)

    Min-Heap (Priority Queue): Sensor data is fed into a heap. Any value crossing a "Danger Threshold" is moved to the root for $O(1)$ access by the emergency shutdown system.
