Smart Forest Fire Detection System

A forest is divided into zones, and each zone has sensors.
We treat the forest as a graph and the fire behavior as a pathfinding and pattern detection problem.

SDG Alignment

  • SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities and Communities
  • SDG 13 – Climate Action
  • SDG 15 – Life on Land

Implementation Steps (ADs)

  • The entire forest is represented as a graph (BFS/DFS) - Nodes as Sensor locations, Edges as Connectivity between sensor nodes, Weights as Distance, vegetation difficulty, or fire spread time
  • Priority Queue - Used to prioritize highest temperature nodes, areas with maximum smoke, fastest fire-spreading zones
  • Circular Queue - Used to store continuous sensor readings
  • Binary Search Tree - Used to maintain sorted order of risk levels
  • Dijkstra’s Algorithm - Used for finding shortest path for firefighters and nearest water source location
  • Minimum Spanning Tree (Prim's) - Used to connect sensors with minimum wiring cost, reduce communication links, Optimize maintenance routes

Impact :

Using data structures and algorithms—like sensor data trees, graph-based alert networks, and pattern-detection algorithms—the smart fire detection system identifies fires early and sends alerts quickly. This reduces response time, prevents large-scale forest damage, and protects wildlife, nearby communities, and natural resources. Overall, it creates a safer, more resilient, and environmentally sustainable forest ecosystem.

System Requirements

  • Hotel & Resort Booking Optimization
  • Tourism Recommendation System
  • Transport & Route Optimization
  • Digital Ticketing & Pass Management

Implementation Steps (ADs)

  • AVL Tree - To sort price list
  • Hash Map - To store booking details
  • Greedy Algorithm - To allocate best available room quickly
  • Backtracking - To handle overbooking scenarios

Smart Water And Electricity System

SDG Alignment

  • SDG 6 – Clean Water and Sanitation
  • SDG 7 – Affordable and Clean Energy
  • SDG 9 – Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure
  • SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities and Communities

Implementation Steps (ADs)

Smart Water Management system :

  • Model water pipelines as a Directed Weighted Graph
  • Use Floyd–Warshall Algorithm to detect inefficient water routes and compute all-pair shortest flow paths
  • Apply Union–Find (Disjoint Set) to identify connected and disconnected water supply regions
  • Store water consumption logs in a Binary Search Tree (BST) for fast range queries

Smart Electricity Distribution System :

  • Represent electrical grid circuits as a Graph with weighted edges for voltage/resistance
  • Use Bellman–Ford Algorithm to detect power fluctuations or negative-cycle faults in the grid
  • Apply Segment Trees to manage and query electricity usage across different time intervals
  • Store outage reports and repair tasks in a Circular Queue for efficient rotation and handling

Impact :

The integrated system improves resource efficiency by reducing leakages, energy loss, and service interruptions. Real-time monitoring ensures reliable delivery, while optimized distribution lowers operational costs and supports sustainable city growth. Together, they make essential utilities smarter, cleaner, and more resilient.
